About the Mission

What is the Bug Bounty Challenge?

A flagship cybersecurity bug bounty competition where participants hunt for vulnerabilities, exploit real security flaws, and submit professional vulnerability reports.

🛰️
The Event

Bug Bounty Challenge is a cybersecurity vulnerability hunting competition hosted by CyberZee, the cybersecurity club of SRM University, Delhi-NCR, Sonepat. Part of the annual technical fest Verge 2026. Participants will test intentionally vulnerable applications and systems to discover security flaws. The goal is not just exploitation but also writing clear and professional vulnerability reports — just like real-world bug bounty programs.

🌌
The Theme

Inspired by Interstellar — traversing the unknown, bending the rules of the digital cosmos, and finding signal in the noise. This isn't just a CTF. It's an expedition into the depths of cyberspace. Do you have the clearance to go beyond?

⚡
Why Participate?

Compete against teams from across the country. Build real-world cybersecurity skills, earn recognition, and connect with a growing community of ethical hackers and security professionals. All skill levels welcome — from first-timers to seasoned CTF players.

🏆
Prizes & Recognition

Winner gets cash prizes, certificates, and swags. All participants receive a participation certificate. Outstanding vulnerability reports may also receive special recognition.. Winners get featured on the CyberZee hall of fame and gain direct visibility within our partner network.

Protocols & Clearance

Rules & Eligibility

Eligibility
  • Open to all students — UG, PG, and school level.
  • Participants from any institution across India can compete.
  • Individual members allowed.
  • No prior Bug Bounty experience required — beginners welcome.
  • Registration through Unstop only (link in nav & CTA).
Competition Rules
  • Participants must submit detailed vulnerability reports explaining the issue and impact.
  • Duplicate submissions will not be counted.
  • Do not attack infrastructure outside the defined scope.
  • Automated mass scanning tools may be restricted.
  • Follow responsible disclosure guidelines.
  • Decisions of the CyberZee organizing team are final.
